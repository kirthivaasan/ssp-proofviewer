Welcome to the CryptoZoo!

A place where state separating proofs (SSPs) live.

State-separating proofs SSPs are a style to define cryptographic properties and in particular to structure their proofs. The CryptoZoo proof viewer allows you to engage with proofs interactively, find relevant information quickly, hide information that isn't interesting to you at the moment and much more. Want to check out how it works?

There are currently three case studies available:

  • IND-CPA: Equivalence of Semantic Security.

    Simulation-based security notions for symmetric encryption state that the adversary should not learn more than some ideal leakage and that everything the adversary can do when given a ciphertext can also be done when only given the ideal leakage, but not the ciphertext. While different views on ideal leakage are possible, the minimal approach is to leak the length of the message that is encrypted, since an adversary can infer the length of the message from the length of the ciphertext. Simulation-based notions which leak the length of the message are typically equivalent to their game-based counterparts.

    In this case study, we provide a proof that indistinguishability under chosen plaintext attacks (IND-CPA) security in its Real-or-Zeroes formulation is equivalent to a simulation-based formulation where the simulator receives only the length of the message m. The proof uses the following definitions:

    • Indistinguishability chosen plaintext attack (IND-CPA)
    • Simulation-based IND-CPA

  • GGM PRF-from-PRG: We give a proof for the famous GGM Tree construction for constant depth.

    Goldreich, Goldwasser and Micali introduced the notion of pseudo-random functions and provided a construction of a pseudorandom function based on a length-doubling pseudorandom generator. The proof is commonly include in courses on the foundations of cryptography and contained, e.g., in Chapter 3.6.2 (Theorem 3.6.6) of the Foundations of Cryptography I textbook by Goldreich. The construction is naturally amenable to visualization: It structures PRG instances into a binary tree and the left halves and right halves of a PRG output become the input to the PRG instances on the next tree layer, until reaching the leaf layer. The proof uses the following definitions

    • (Length Doubling) Pseudo-Random Generators (PRG)
    • Pseudo-Random Functions (PRF)

  • Garbling Security of Yao's Protocol: Brzuska and Oechsner formalized security of Yao's Garblind Scheme in the framework of SSPs.
